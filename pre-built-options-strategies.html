Options strategies are plans that traders use to combine different options (call and/or put) to achieve profits. Options strategies also keep you hedged so that the losses are in control.

Let us take an example, imagine you believe a stock or index price will rise. You might use a "bull call spread" strategy, where you buy a call option and sell another call option with a higher strike price than the stock or index current spot price. This way, you can profit from the stock's increase while limiting your risk.

Another example is a "bear put spread," used when you think a stock's or index price will drop. You buy a put option and sell another put option with a lower strike price than the current spot price of stock or index. This strategy lets you profit from the decline while managing potential losses.

Options strategies help traders make money whether the market goes up, down, or stays the same, by carefully planning which options to buy or sell based on their market outlook.
